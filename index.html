The Library of Babel

By Jorge Luis Borges

Once I am dead, there will be no lack of pious hands to throw me over the railing;
my grave will be the fathomless air;
my body will sink endlessly and decay and dissolve in the wind generated by the fall, which is infinite.
I say that the Library is unending.
The idealists argue that the hexagonal rooms are a necessary form of absolute space or,
at least of our intuition of space.
